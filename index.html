Paragraph 2

After watching the talk of Tanya DePass, it made me realized how minority groups are being underrepresented in the game developer’s community. I did not know of this problem until I saw this video. Tanya did bring up a great point of how a lot of video games have very similar plots or background stories. Tanya explained the reason for this is because many game developers are white men. Development teams don’t have diversity and therefore don’t have much creativity beyond of their own personal experiences. The main characters of many games are white males and when I think of all the games, I played in the past it shocks me how I didn’t notice it before. I believe Tanya is doing a great job raising awareness and trying to get underrepresent communities more opportunities to land in the industry through her organization and other ones.

Paragraph 3

Sarian’s article of Ethical Self-Reflection in Papers, Please explores the ethical dilemmas of the game “Papers, Please” and tries to understand how people can commit atrocities. It explains that people who are put in situations like the ones in the game are following orders and doing their job. It explains Max Weber’s notion of rational authority in how politics is organized in modern bureaucracies as regimented and regulated processes, forcing people to do what is required of them.
